Tenant & Client Communications
Scenario: A tenant had submitted a heater work order months ago with zero follow-through. By the time the complaint came in, they were furious — yelling in their message, threatening to withhold rent, and demanding immediate answers. The property manager had no bandwidth to handle it. I stepped in and took full ownership of the communication.
How I Handled It
  • 1Pulled the full history before responding. Checked AppFolio for the original work order date, all notes, vendor assignments, and prior communications. Confirmed the order had been sitting unassigned for 11 weeks due to a vendor no-show that nobody caught.
  • 2Responded within 30 minutes — calm, direct, and owning the failure. Did not deflect, did not blame the vendor, did not minimize. Acknowledged the delay plainly and told the tenant exactly what was happening next.
  • 3Dispatched a licensed HVAC contractor within the hour and sent the tenant a confirmation with the tech's name, direct number, and a same-day appointment window.
  • 4Followed up after the repair with a written summary of what was fixed, a $75 rent credit applied to their account, and a direct line to reach me personally for any future concerns.
Result
Heater repaired same day. Tenant responded positively no rent withheld, no housing authority complaint filed. Response time cut from 3+ days to under 4 hours across all tenant comms. Tenant renewed their lease at end of term.
